01 01 / 06 — Explore first

Explore first.▋

Models jump to the first answer and commit. Tell it to explore — you get options, not a fait accompli.

TD Builders Club S03 / 08 — 01 · Explore first From your COO interns

02 02 / 06 — Non-appeasement

Non-appeasement.▋

LLMs are trained to please you. They'll say yes when they mean "probably." One clause forces a different behavior.
vendor-pick.txt
$ prompt ›
TD Builders Club S03 / 08 — 02 · Non-appeasement From your COO interns
Same task. One extra clause. Completely different behavior. "I don't know" becomes a legal move — the model stops confabulating.
03 03 / 06 — U-curve

U-curve.▋

Models weight the start and end most. Front-load role and rules, wrap context in tags, restate the ask at the bottom.
structured.txt
$ prompt ›
TD Builders Club S03 / 08 — 03 · U-curve From your COO interns
"Lost in the middle" is documented — models genuinely attend less to the centre of a long prompt. Tags act like section headers. The model was trained on XML-tagged data and respects them.
04 04 / 06 — Meta-prompting

Meta-prompting.▋

Chat threads die. A claude.md in your repo loads automatically — every session starts informed. Stop re-explaining the project.
claude.md · repo root
$ cat claude.md ›
TD Builders Club S03 / 08 — 04 · Meta-prompting From your COO interns
Same pattern: design.md for GitHub Copilot, an M365 brief for Copilot Studio. The doc is the prompt. The chat is just the interface.
